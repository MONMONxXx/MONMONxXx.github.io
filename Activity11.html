OPERATING SYSTEM (OS)

WHAT IS OPERATING SYSTEM (OS)

An Operating System (OS) is a software that manages computer hardware and software resources and provides common services for computer programs. It acts as a bridge between the hardware and the software and allows different applications to communicate with the computer's hardware. There are different types of Operating Systems, each with its unique features and functions. In this article, we will explore some of the popular Operating Systems and their platform technologies.

THE INTRODUCTION TO OS

Windows: Windows is the most popular Operating System in the world, developed and owned by Microsoft. It is widely used in desktop and laptop computers and is known for its user-friendly interface and compatibility with various applications. Windows uses the NT kernel, which is responsible for managing memory, processes, and device drivers. Windows supports a wide range of hardware and software, making it a popular choice for personal and business use.

Linux: Linux is a free and open-source Operating System that is widely used in servers, supercomputers, and mobile devices. It is built on the UNIX model and uses the Linux kernel, which is responsible for managing memory, processes, and device drivers. Linux offers a high level of flexibility and customization, making it popular among developers and tech-savvy users. Linux also supports a wide range of software applications, including those designed for Windows.

macOS: macOS is an Operating System developed by Apple Inc. and is exclusively used on Apple's hardware, such as MacBook, iMac, and Mac Pro. It is known for its sleek design and high-quality graphics, making it popular among creative professionals. macOS is built on a UNIX foundation and uses the Darwin kernel, which is responsible for managing memory, processes, and device drivers. macOS supports a wide range of software applications, including those designed for Windows.

In conclusion, Operating Systems come in different forms, catering to different needs. The choice of Operating System depends on the requirements of the user, such as user-friendliness, compatibility, and customization. It's essential to choose the right Operating System that meets your needs, as it can affect the user experience, performance, and security of your device.
